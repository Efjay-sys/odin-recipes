Why choose Lasagne?

Ancient Roots:

The term "lasagna" itself may have roots in ancient Greek, with "laganon" being a flatbread or pasta dish. Romans adopted a similar dish called "lagane," which likely inspired the word "lasagne".

Medieval Italy:

The lasagna we know today, with its layered structure, developed in Naples during the Middle Ages. The earliest recorded recipe for lasagna is from the 14th century.

Variations and Evolution:

Lasagna became popular across Italy, with variations emerging in different regions. For example, the "Lasagne di Carnevale" from Naples features sausage, meatballs, and hard-boiled eggs. "Lasagne al Forno," from Emilia-Romagna, uses a rich ragù sauce and béchamel.

Global Popularity:

Italian immigrants brought their lasagna recipes to America, leading to further variations and adaptations. Today, lasagna is a beloved dish worldwide, enjoyed in countless flavors and forms.

Ingredients

  • Base: Ground beef, ground pork, Italian sausage, or a combination.
  • Vegetables: Onion, garlic, carrots, celery, and bell peppers are common additions.
  • Tomato Base: Crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, and sometimes canned whole tomatoes.
  • Seasoning: Herbs like basil, oregano, parsley, and spices like garlic salt, salt, pepper, and red wine.

  • Ricotta Cheese: Provides a creamy and flavorful base for the cheese layers.
  • Mozzarella Cheese: Adds gooey and melty goodness.
  • Parmesan Cheese: Enhances the flavor with its savory and salty notes.

  • Lasagna Noodles: Forms the pasta layers.
  • Olive Oil: Used for cooking the meat and vegetables.
  • Eggs: Used to bind the ricotta cheese mixture.
  • Optional: White wine, beef stock, Worcestershire sauce, and sugar can be added to enhance the flavor of the meat sauce.

Steps to make Lasagne

  1. Heat the oven to 400ºF
  2. Brown the beef and onion
  3. Prepare the baking dish and assemble the meat sauce
  4. Begin layering the lasagna
  5. Continue layering the lasagna
  6. Bake the lasagna for 1 hour
  7. Sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella and finish baking
  8. Cool the lasagna for 15 minutes
